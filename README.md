# Freedom of Information Act Request: Records Concerning Reported “Revival” Events Connected to the 250th Anniversary of the Declaration of Independence

## What is this?

This is an educational Freedom of Information Act request template concerning reported “revival” events connected to the United States’ 250th anniversary commemoration.

## Why should I care?

Public records requests help people understand how government agencies plan, fund, review, coordinate, and communicate about public events. This template gives requesters a structured starting point for seeking records about federal planning, funding, legal review, public messaging, and coordination concerning reported faith-themed or “revival” events tied to the 2026 commemoration.

## What is the claim?

The practical claim is that public oversight requires traceable records. If federal funds, personnel, property, legal review, public messaging, or interagency coordination are involved in public commemorative events with religious or politically adjacent dimensions, a requester should be able to ask for non-exempt, reasonably segregable agency records in a clear and usable way.

## What is this not?

This is not legal advice, a filed FOIA request, or a guarantee that any agency possesses responsive records. It is an educational template. Requesters should adapt it to the receiving agency, requester status, available facts, source citations, fee limits, and current law before use.

## Who is this for?

This template is for journalists, researchers, advocates, civic organizations, public-interest requesters, policy staff, lawyers, students, and members of the public seeking a practical FOIA starting point.

## How was this done?

The template is structured as a FOIA letter with search terms, likely offices and custodians, requested record categories, preservation language, rolling-production language, fee-waiver language, expedited-processing language, format-and-delivery language, referral language, and requester contact placeholders.
